FAQ

Here at We Did It Global we get lots of questions from our artists regarding various aspects of our service. To make it easier, we decided to make a collection of the most frequently asked questions and give you the answers below. Check them out, and maybe you’ll find the answer that you’ve been looking for.

How long is the contract?

It is 10 years contract generally, members are allowed to exit on completion of year 3, year 5 or year 7 paying an exit fee.

Why do you keep 30% from all earning?

We do not take any commission from public performing royalties nor collect them in Sri Lanka. 100% (less any admin fee by the collector) public performing royalties should be paid to you by the relevant CMO that collect it for you in Sri Lanka We have provided over 150 authors and composers a free membership to register them worldwide, to register you and your songs to make them eligible to earn required specialist acknowledged as well as international operations that require adequate staffing, resources and funding. Only way we can make our investment back is by keeping a commission from the earning we made possible for you. Company also will recreate your songs to make further earning potentials and keep them popular in the public. We also have tax obligations and other commitments such as licensing, monitoring and maintain our services and products which all require funds to operate correctly to earn on your rights.

Why does my song say it’s ‘pending’?

This usually means we’re in the process of getting the song registered at the PROs/CMOs. Once you’ve added a new song to the website, we need to send this information off to the different collection societies in order to set up registrations.

I’ve not been paid for my radio/tv play.

Radio and television royalties (Please do not get confused with licence and royalty pay) should come through automatically about 9-12 months after the date of the broadcast and, because of this, we are unable to proactively make claims for broadcasts like we can for other platforms. However, we can follow up with the CMO if you haven’t been paid for specific broadcasts after 12 months – that’s why it’s a good idea to keep your We Did It Global account up to date with any broadcasts you’re aware of.

How can I claim royalties from YouTube?

If your music is receiving substantial views on YouTube or if you know it is being used without your permission on the platform then please email info@wediditglobal.com to ensure it has been delivered to YouTube’s Content ID platform, ensure it is protected and where possible royalties are being generated. What the difference between Royalty Pay and Licencing Pay Royalty pay is that generate after a song been broadcasted whereas Licencing Pay required to be paid before using the songs been broadcasted known as “Blanket Licensing” or before attempting to remake/reproduced such as in reality TV programs

Why haven’t I been paid for the use in public yet?

In Sri Lanka there is no proper mechanism to collect royalties from public performing yet. Crucially lack of active Collective Management Organisation for authors and composers as well as for the publishers such as We Did It Global. Once a CMO established and operational as a rough guide, royalties from smaller venues (pubs and small venues) can take 6-9 months to come through; royalties from larger venues (academies, for example) usually take 9-12 months to come through and royalties from overseas venues and festivals can take a year or more to come through. It’s important to note that this waiting time begins when We Did It Global creates your IPI number from the COMPASS (our COMs) and register your songs in the worldwide catalogue.

What’s my IPI number?

IPI number know as Interested Party Information if the unique ID as an Author or composer. This number is created for you by the Collective Management Organisation and all your music and lyrics are registered under the IPI number for easy reference and royalty collection.

How do I get paid?

You will be paid your share of any performance royalties worldwide except Sri Lanka directly by the COMPASS, your CMO. Any other earning from mechanical royalties We Did It Global makes distributions quarterly in January, April, July and October. You will receive an email statement from us if the balance of your account totals over LKR 1000. We will pay out any amount above RS 1000 directly to the payment information you have provided us in your name.

Why don’t you automatically collect my Neighbouring Rights royalties?

Neighbouring Rights refer to the royalties owed for the broadcast of the master recordings. As a publisher we do not traditionally represent the master recordings of our artists. This usually falls under the role of the label; however, it is becoming more common for artists to retain their master rights and We Did It Global has a separate label when this is the case, we can represent the master rights holder share and collect their Neighbouring Rights royalties on our clients’ behalf. Apparently my song got played on a TV show, was this through We Did It Global? The way TV should works in the Sri Lanka is under blanket license deals, so any producer can use any track at any-time as long as the tracks are registered with the CMO in Sri Lanka which is yet to establish or to proactively licence use of songs on their original publication.

Can I claim royalties for a cover song I have created?

We are unable to collect royalties for cover songs. As a publisher, we collect for any original compositions on behalf of the original songwriters.
